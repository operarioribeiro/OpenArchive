# [Open Archive](https://github.com/operarioribeiro/OpenArchive)

## Open Archive

**Open Archive** is the application of open source technologies and archivologic biblioteconomic library information science (LIS) to the management of files in any size and degree of complexity. It is based on the [open science](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_science) research [Open Archive Management](https://www.academia.edu/144448058/Open_Archive).

![image](https://github.com/operarioribeiro/OpenArchive/blob/main/Open%20Archive.png)

##  Open Methodology:

1. **[Open Archive](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archive):** [Archivologic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archival_science) [library information science (LIS)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Library_and_information_science) [file](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_file) [management](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Management).
2.  **[Open File System](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File_system):** [Information retrieval (IR)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Information_retrieval) [library classification method](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Library_classification#Methods_or_systems) [document classification](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Document_classification) [open standard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_standard).
3. **[Open Access](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open-access_repository):** [File system permissions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File-system_permissions) [access-control list (ACL)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Access-control_list).
4. **[Open Storage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Storage):** [Collections management](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Collections_management) [warehouse](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Warehouse) and [data warehouse (DW)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data_warehouse) [computer storage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_storage_device) [open infra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_Infrastructure_Architecture_method).
5. **[Open Metadata](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metadata):** [Open data](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_data) [metadata registry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metadata_registry?wprov=srpw1_2).
6. **[Open Files](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_file_format):** [Open file format](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_file_format) [semantic web](https://www.w3.org/2001/sw/wiki/Main_Page) [compliant ontology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ontology_alignment) [controlled vocabulary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Controlled_vocabulary) [file naming](https://en.wikiversity.org/wiki/File_naming).
7. **[Open Directory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Directory_(computing)):** [Directory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Directory_structure) [file structure](https://mitcommlab.mit.edu/broad/commkit/file-structure/)    [taxonomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taxonomy).

##  Open Filename:

Files Names are to be organized in the following way to improve their [human readability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human-readable_medium_and_data), [accessibility](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Accessibility), and [self-explanation](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/self-explanatory):

**Historical Files Name:** Year.Month. Name of The File - Author of the File.[filetype](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_file_format)

**Historical Files Example:** 2025.10. Open Archive - Felipe Ribeiro.odt

**Hierarchical Files Name:** Number. Name of the File - Author of the File.[filetype](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_file_format)

**Hierarchical Files Example:** 1. Open Archive - Felipe Ribeiro.odt

##  Open Directory Structure:

Folders and Files being ordered by the year and month dates or hierarchy numbers in front of their name allow for their better organization. I propose the following [data structure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data_structure) [alignment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data_structure_alignment):

- **1. Personal:**
- **1.1. Documents:** Birth certificate, identity register, passport, driver\'s license, electoral title, healthcare card, military register, professional union registers, etc.
- **1.2. Credentials:** Individual certificates.
  - **1.2.1. Clearance:** Background checks, criminal records, debts assessment, legal duties to fulfill assessments, sociopsychological capacities assessments, signed ethical pledges, conflicts of interest disclosures, languages assessment, technical proficiency assessment, etc.
  - **1.2.2. Degrees:** Documents from pre-school, primary school, childhood courses, high school, adolescence courses, and undergraduate degrees.
  - **1.2.3. Post-Graduations:** Master of arts (M.A.), master of science (M.S.), master of education (M.Ed.), master of divinities (M.Div.), doctorate (Dr.), post-honoris doctor (Ph.D), and other strictu sensu graduations diplomas and track-records with their due credits table.
  - **1.2.4. Specializations:** Accredited latu sensu diplomas and historical track records with their due credits table.
  - **1.2.5. Experience:** Proofs of works like previous contracts and official documents.
  - **1.2.6. Recognitions:** Condecorations, doctor honoris causa (D.H.C.) Awards, badges, grants, etc.
  - **1.2.7. Recommendations:** Personal and institutional endorsement.
- **1.3. Legal:** Preferably secured ledgers under encryption.
  - **1.3.1. Accounting:** Monthly balance budget along its due payments bills.
  - **1.3.2. Contracts:** Hiring, marriage certificate, personal insurances, job arrangements, work contracts, and other legal documents.
  - **1.3.3. Audit:** Reports, litigations, and other legal cases.
- **1.4. Medical:** Vaccines card, previous conditions diagnosis, emergency treatment necessities documents, and other official documents.
- **1.5. Interface:** Personalization for the computer operational system (OS) itself.
  - **1.5.1. Fonts.**
  - **1.5.2. Wallpapers.**
  - **1.5.3. Multimedia:** Icons, skins, sounds, animations, mods, softwares, etc.
- **1.6. Websites:** With at least one biographical personal page.
- **1.7. Letters.**
- **2. Institutions:** With one folder for each institution one works with.
- **3. Research:** With one folder for each project and subject one studies to organize all external images, audio, and video.
- **4. Writings:** Publications, academic papers, news articles, magazine texts, web log (blog) hypertexts, etc.
- **5. Projects:** Non-institutional projects, like forked GitHub repositories.
- **6. Heritage:** Self produced artworks, products, and their due provenance certifications.
- **7. Reports:** Memorial video-photographic folders ordered historically.
